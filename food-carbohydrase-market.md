<p><p>The food carbohydrase market is segmented into different types based on the specific enzymes they produce. Lactase is responsible for breaking down lactose in milk products, and pectinase helps break down pectin in fruits. Other carbohydrase enzymes may include invertase, maltase, and sucrase, each with their own unique functions in breaking down carbohydrates into simpler sugars for easier digestion and absorption.</p></p>
