<p><p>3D bioprinting in the medical market involves using various technologies to create 3D structures from living cells. Magnetic 3D bioprinting uses magnetic forces to position cells into desired patterns. Laser-assisted bioprinting utilizes lasers to precisely build tissue structures layer by layer. Inkjet 3D bioprinting involves ejecting cells through a nozzle onto a substrate to form tissues. Microextrusion 3D bioprinting uses a small needle to deposit bioinks and create complex tissue structures. These technologies offer great potential in regenerative medicine and drug development.</p></p>
