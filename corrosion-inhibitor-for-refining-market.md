<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors for refining can be classified into two main types: organic corrosion inhibitors and inorganic corrosion inhibitors. Organic corrosion inhibitors are typically composed of carbon-based molecules that form a protective film on the metal surface, preventing corrosion. Inorganic corrosion inhibitors, on the other hand, are composed of non-carbon-based compounds such as salts or oxides that work by either forming a protective barrier or altering the chemical environment to inhibit corrosion. Both types of corrosion inhibitors are used in the refining industry to protect equipment and infrastructure from corrosion damage.</p></p>
