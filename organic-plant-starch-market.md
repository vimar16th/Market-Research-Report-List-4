<p><p>Organic plant starch market types include corn starch, potato starch, and cassava starch. Corn starch is derived from the endosperm of corn kernels and is commonly used as a thickening agent in various food products. Potato starch is extracted from potatoes and is used in gluten-free recipes as a thickening agent. Cassava starch is obtained from the cassava root and is often used in baking and as a binder in food products. Other market types may include arrowroot starch, tapioca starch, and wheat starch.</p></p>
