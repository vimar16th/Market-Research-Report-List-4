Absorbable sutures are designed to break down over time and are typically used for internal tissues that heal quickly. Non-absorbable sutures are made of materials that do not break down and are used for external wounds or tissues that require longer healing times. Both types play a crucial role in wound closure and surgical procedures, offering healthcare professionals a range of options to meet specific patient needs.</p></p>
