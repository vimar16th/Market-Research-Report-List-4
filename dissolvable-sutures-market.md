<p><p>Dissolvable sutures, also known as absorbable sutures, come in two main types: Natural Dissolvable Sutures and Synthetic Dissolvable Sutures. Natural dissolvable sutures are made from materials like catgut or silk that break down in the body over time. On the other hand, synthetic dissolvable sutures are composed of materials like polyglycolic acid or polyglactin that are designed to be absorbed by the body. Both types of dissolvable sutures have their own advantages and are used in various medical procedures to close wounds.</p></p>
