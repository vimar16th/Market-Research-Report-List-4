<p><p>Non-nutritive sweeteners are commonly divided into two main types: artificial sweeteners and natural sweeteners. Artificial sweeteners are synthetic sugar substitutes that provide sweetness without the calories found in sugar. Some examples include saccharin, aspartame, and sucralose. Natural sweeteners, on the other hand, are derived from plants or other natural sources and are often perceived as healthier alternatives to artificial sweeteners. Examples include stevia, monk fruit, and honey. Both types of sweeteners are used in a variety of food and beverage products to provide sweetness without added calories.</p></p>
