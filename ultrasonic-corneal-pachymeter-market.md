<p><p>Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeters are used in ophthalmology to measure the thickness of the cornea. There are two main types of Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeters on the market: Contact and Non-Contact. Contact Ultrasonic Pachymeters require direct contact with the cornea to take measurements, while Non-Contact Ultrasonic Pachymeters use air-pulse technology to measure the corneal thickness without touching the eye. Both types of devices provide essential information for diagnosing and treating eye conditions such as glaucoma and corneal diseases.</p></p>
