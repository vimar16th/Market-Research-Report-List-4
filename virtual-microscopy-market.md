<p><p>Virtual microscopy refers to the digitization of traditional glass slides, allowing for viewing and analyzing specimens on a computer screen. There are two main types of virtual microscopy markets: Brightfield and Fluorescence. Brightfield microscopy involves illuminating a sample with white light, while Fluorescence microscopy uses specific wavelengths of light to excite fluorescent molecules in the sample. Both types offer high-resolution imaging and the ability to easily share and collaborate on digital images, making them valuable tools in research, education, and diagnostics.</p></p>
