<p><p>Confectioners sugar, commonly known as powdered sugar, is finely ground granulated sugar mixed with cornstarch to prevent clumping. Rock sugar, on the other hand, consists of large, crystallized sugar pieces that are often used as a sweetener for beverages like tea or coffee. Both types of sugar cater to different culinary needs and preferences, with powdered sugar being preferred for its fine texture and rock sugar for its unique appearance and slow-dissolving properties.</p></p>
