<p><p>Portable hemostasis analyzers are devices used to test blood clotting ability in patients. There are two main types of detection methods used in these analyzers: optical detection and mechanical detection. Optical detection relies on light absorption or scattering to determine blood clotting time, whereas mechanical detection measures changes in pressure or motion to assess clot formation. Both types of detection methods are crucial for accurate and reliable analysis of blood clotting disorders in portable hemostasis analyzers.</p></p>
