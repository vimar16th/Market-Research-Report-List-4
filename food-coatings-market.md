<p><p>Food coatings can be classified into two main types: dry and liquid. Dry coatings typically consist of powders or crumbs that adhere to the surface of the food through various methods such as breading or dredging. On the other hand, liquid coatings are made up of liquid ingredients such as batter or marinades that are applied to the food before cooking. Both dry and liquid coatings serve the purpose of enhancing the flavor, texture, and appearance of the food product.</p></p>
