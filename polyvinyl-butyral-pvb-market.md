<p><strong>The Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Alkaline Alcoholysis</li><li>Acidic Alcoholysis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB) can be produced through two main types of processes: Alkaline Alcoholysis and Acidic Alcoholysis. In Alkaline Alcoholysis, PVB is obtained by reacting polyvinyl acetate with butyraldehyde in the presence of an alkaline catalyst. On the other hand, Acidic Alcoholysis involves the reaction of polyvinyl acetate with butanol in the presence of an acidic catalyst to form PVB. Both processes result in the production of high-quality PVB, which is widely used in industries such as automotive, construction, and packaging.</p></p>
