<p><p>Scanning Probe Microscopy (SPM) is a powerful technique used for imaging surfaces at the nanoscale. There are three main types of SPM: Atomic Force Microscopes (AFM) which use a sharp tip to scan the surface and measure forces, Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) which map surface properties based on electron tunneling, and Near-field Scanning Optical Microscopes (NSOM) which use a tiny aperture to probe surface features optically. Each type of SPM has its own unique advantages and applications in the field of nanotechnology.</p></p>
