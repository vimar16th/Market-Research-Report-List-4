<p><p>The field crops drip irrigation emitter market consists of various types such as drippers, pressure pump systems, and drip tube systems. Drippers are small devices that deliver water directly to the plant roots. Pressure pump systems use a pump to create pressure for water flow. Drip tube systems consist of tubes with emitters spaced evenly along their length, delivering water consistently across the field. Each type of emitter is tailored to meet specific irrigation needs and preferences of farmers.</p></p>

<p><p>Field Crops Drip Irrigation Emitters are used in both surface and subsurface applications in the market. Surface drip irrigation emitters are placed above the soil surface, delivering water directly to the root zone of the crops. Subsurface drip irrigation emitters are placed below the soil surface, delivering water directly to the root zone without wetting the foliage. Both applications help improve water efficiency, reduce water wastage, and ensure precise delivery of water to the crops, leading to increased crop productivity.</p></p>
